Fossils are super important in paleontology because they give us a peek into the past. They're like time capsules, preserving ancient life forms and ecosystems. Without fossils, we wouldn't know squat about dinosaurs, early humans, or ancient plants. Plus, they help us understand evolution and how life on Earth has changed over millions of years.
